# High School English Grammar — Key Points for CSAT (수능 영어)

## 1. Verb Tenses

### Present Perfect vs Simple Past

Use **present perfect** when the time is not specified or when the action connects to the present:
- I **have visited** Paris three times. (experience, no specific time)
- She **has just finished** her homework. (recent past with present relevance)

Use **simple past** when the time is clearly stated or in the past:
- I **visited** Paris in 2019. (specific time)
- He **called** me yesterday.

### Since vs For

- **Since** is followed by a point in time: since 2020, since last Monday, since I was a child
- **For** is followed by a period of time: for three years, for a long time, for two weeks

Example:
- I have studied English **for** five years.
- She has lived here **since** 2018.

## 2. Relative Pronouns: That vs Which

### "That"
- Used for both people and things.
- Used after superlatives, ordinals, or words like "all", "every", "any", "only".
- Cannot be used after a comma (no non-restrictive use).
- Cannot follow a preposition directly.

Examples:
- This is the best movie **that** I have ever seen.
- All the books **that** she recommends are helpful.

### "Which"
- Used for things and animals (not people as subject/object in restrictive clauses).
- Can be used after a comma for non-restrictive (additional information) clauses.
- Can follow a preposition.

Examples:
- The book, **which** was published in 2020, became a bestseller.
- This is the topic about **which** we are talking.

## 3. Subjunctive Mood (가정법)

### Hypothetical Present/Future (가정법 과거)
If + past tense, would/could/might + base verb
- If I **were** a bird, I **would fly** freely. (Note: "were" for all subjects)
- If she **had** more time, she **could help** us.

### Hypothetical Past (가정법 과거완료)
If + had + past participle, would/could/might + have + past participle
- If I **had studied** harder, I **would have passed** the exam.

### Mixed Conditional
If + had + past participle (past condition), would + base verb (present result)
- If I **had taken** the job, I **would be** rich now.

## 4. Reading Comprehension Strategies (빈칸 추론)

### CSAT English Blank-Filling Strategy

1. **Read the whole passage first** to grasp the main idea and tone.
2. **Identify the topic sentence** — usually the first or second sentence of each paragraph.
3. **Look for logical connectors** around the blank:
   - Contrast: however, but, although, despite → opposite meaning expected
   - Cause/Result: therefore, thus, as a result → logical consequence
   - Addition: moreover, furthermore, in addition → same direction idea
4. **Eliminate wrong options** — cross out choices that contradict the passage.
5. **Paraphrase matching**: the correct answer often paraphrases a key idea from earlier in the passage.

### Common Blank Patterns
- Abstract noun at the end of the passage (e.g., "This phenomenon is called ___.")
- Adjective describing the main concept
- Relationship between two concepts (A enables B, A prevents B)

## 6. Sentence Order (글의 순서 배열) Tips

1. Find the sentence that introduces a new noun or concept — this is often first.
2. A sentence starting with "This", "These", "It" refers back to the previous sentence.
3. Logical connectors: "However" signals contrast after a positive statement.
4. If an example is given ("For example", "For instance"), the claim comes before it.
5. The concluding sentence often uses "therefore", "in conclusion", "ultimately".
